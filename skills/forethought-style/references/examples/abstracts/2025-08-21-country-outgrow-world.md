# Could One Country Outgrow the Rest of the World After AGI?

When economies grow exponentially, relative sizes between nations remain constant. However, after AGI development, economic growth may become superexponentialâ€”accelerating continuously over time. Under such conditions, "an initial economic lead becomes bigger and bigger over time. The leader eventually produces >99% of global output."

The analysis identifies two primary pathways to dominance: the Superexponential Growth Argument and the Grabbing New Resources Argument. The first requires a country or bloc controlling >50% of GDP to prevent technological diffusion among competitors. The second involves seizing unclaimed resources, particularly space-based solar energy, to convert temporary advantages into permanent dominance.

A critical constraint is technological diffusion. While the leading AI nation could theoretically outgrow the world, doing so "only if it makes a concerted and well-coordinated effort to prevent technological diffusion." Such coordination might occur through US-allied export controls on AI chips or following a coup by power-seeking actors.
