**3/6:** Assuming we get our act together on cleanup and tracking, and we don't see many more kinetic anti-sat weapons, I think it's fairly unlikely we'll sleepwalk into Kessler syndrome so bad that it becomes impractical to put sats in preferential orbits.

**4/6:** But what if someone launched debris *into* orbit from Earth? That could make things a lot worse.

In the extreme, huge amounts of debris could even block launches *passing through* orbit. Fortunately I think that's quite unlikely to be practical, especially with better shielding.
